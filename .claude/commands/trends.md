# Baymax LinkedIn Trend Finder

## Purpose
Find and analyse what's trending on LinkedIn so Cameron can create timely, relevant content that rides existing momentum rather than starting from zero.

## Cameron's Niches
Always investigate trends across ALL of these lenses:

| Niche | Keywords to search |
|---|---|
| **AI & Automation** | AI agents, automation, AI for business, ChatGPT, Claude, AI tools, workflow automation, AI strategy |
| **Property & Real Estate** | UK property, property tech, PropTech, property investment, HMO, real estate AI, property developers |
| **SMEs & Entrepreneurship** | Small business UK, SME growth, founder life, bootstrapping, scaling, business automation |
| **LinkedIn & Personal Brand** | LinkedIn growth, personal branding, content strategy, thought leadership, building in public |
| **General Hot Topics** | Whatever is dominating LinkedIn right now — tech launches, economic news, workplace culture debates, viral posts |

---

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### STEP 1 — Broad LinkedIn trend scan

Run these web searches in parallel to get a wide view:

1. `"LinkedIn trending topics this week March 2026"`
2. `"LinkedIn viral posts this week 2026"`
3. `"AI automation trending LinkedIn 2026"`
4. `"UK property business trending topics March 2026"`
5. `"SME small business trending LinkedIn UK 2026"`
6. `"LinkedIn content ideas trending March 2026"`

Also search for:
7. `"AI news this week March 2026"` — tech announcements that create LinkedIn conversation
8. `"UK business news this week March 2026"` — economic/policy news SME founders care about

---

### STEP 2 — Deep dive on promising trends

For any trend that looks strong, run follow-up searches to understand:
- **What specifically is being said** — the angle, not just the topic
- **Who's talking about it** — big creators, niche voices, or everyone
- **What format is working** — stories, hot takes, educational breakdowns, carousels
- **How Cameron can add a unique angle** — his AI builder + property niche perspective

---

### STEP 3 — Cross-reference with Cameron's content

Check if any trends connect to:
- InnovaAI's recent projects or wins (check Trello pipeline if relevant)
- Cameron's personal experiences (building Baymax, running an agency, client stories)
- Upcoming events or launches Cameron has planned
- Previous posts Cameron has written (avoid repeating the same angle)

## Rules

1. **Minimum 5 trends, maximum 10** — enough to choose from, not overwhelming
2. **Every trend must have a Cameron-specific angle** — never just say "post about AI". Say exactly what Cameron should say and why it fits his brand.
3. **Heat rating must be honest** — "Hot" = everyone's talking about it now. "Warm" = solid engagement, proven topic. "Rising" = early signs, could blow up.
4. **Hook ideas must follow Cameron's voice rules** — short, punchy, no corporate language. Pattern interrupt style.
5. **Recency matters** — prioritise what's happening THIS week. Flag if a trend is older but still relevant.
6. **UK lens** — Cameron's audience is UK-based. Flag if a trend is US-heavy and suggest how to localise it.
7. **No generic advice** — "Post about AI" is useless. "Post about how you built a client's SOW in 30 minutes using your AI system while competitors take 2 weeks" is useful.
8. **Always end with the Top 3 + Quick-fire ideas** — Cameron should leave with a clear action plan.

## Error Handling

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Search results are thin / outdated | Flag it honestly, expand search to adjacent topics |
| A trend is US-only | Note it and suggest a UK localisation angle |
| Can't find enough trends | Supplement with evergreen high-performers in Cameron's niches |
| Cameron asks about a specific niche only | Focus the report on that niche but still include 1-2 general trends |
