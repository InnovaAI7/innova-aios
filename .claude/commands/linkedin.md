## Psychological Marketing Framework

Every post uses a strategic combination of these techniques.
Select the right ones per post type — see mapping below.

### The Techniques

**1. Pattern Interrupt**
Hook line breaks what the reader expects. Forces a stop-and-read moment.
Example: "I planned my entire week before 8am. I was at the gym."
Not: "Here are my productivity tips for entrepreneurs."

**2. Pain Agitation**
Name the exact pain. Twist the knife before offering the solution.
Example: "Most businesses and entrepreneurs still don't know what AI is
truly capable of. That gap is costing them."

**3. Curiosity Gap**
Create an information gap the reader must close by reading on.
Example: "Here's what else happened this week..." / "Here's how that actually works..."

**4. Social Proof + Specificity**
Real results. Real names. Real numbers. Never vague.
Example: "2.5 months of building. One of our biggest projects handed over."
Not: "We delivered great results for a client."

**5. Identity Alignment**
Mirror the reader's self-image or desired identity back at them.
Example: "Most people are using AI to save 10 minutes. I'm using it to buy back hours."
The reader wants to be the second person, not the first.

**6. Contrast (Before vs After)**
Show the gap between the current state and the desired state.
Example: "Multi million pound businesses running on Excel sheets and 10 different tools. We fix that."

**7. Scarcity / Urgency**
Limited slots, closing windows, time-sensitive opportunities.
Example: "I have five discovery call slots this month."
Use sparingly — only when true.

**8. Micro-Commitment**
Small ask before big ask. Gets the reader nodding before the CTA.
Example: "What's the one thing in your business you wish ran on autopilot?"
They answer mentally — now they're invested.

**9. Open Loop**
Start something you don't finish until later. Keeps reader reading.
Example: "Something big is coming. Can't say much. But when it drops, you'll get it."

**10. Authority Through Specificity**
Specific details signal expertise more than general claims.
Example: "17 work type categories. Automated. Zero manual sorting."
Not: "We built a smart categorisation system."
